A note from the author

Mapping human data does more than satisfy a curiosity. It's a way of legitimizing an aggregated condition. While needing to trade nuance for sweeping hypotheses can be off-putting, the ability to show statistical relationships is hugely valuable as a corroboration of the anecdotal.

Data sources

US Census Bureau Demographic Data, DCP Community Districts, DCP MAPPLUTO, ACS 2020 Census Tracts, DCP Zoning Application Portal.

Caveats

The p-value threshold was chosen as 0.1, which is more lenient than the commonly seen 0.05. In truth, the p-value threshold is arbitrary and relative, not absolute, values should be the focus here. Gaps in regions of the choropleth maps are the result of missing or corrupted data. Gaps in regions of the Manhattan map mean that the neighborhood did not have a statistically significant correlation. Several correlation pairs were insignificant city-wide (3 of 10). These were omitted from the dropdown.
